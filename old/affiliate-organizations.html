Affiliated Organizations

Association of Computing Machinery

ACM is the world's largest educational and scientific computing society. As a member at Texas Tech, I participate in mock interviews, cyber security workshops, internship panels, company info sessions, and so much more. ACM has helps me to prepare professionally and learn more about computer science in the industry.

Software Development Club

Software Development Club allows students to get hands on experience by working in teams on various projects. This semester, I was a member of the iOS development team. Our goal was to develop a iPhone application that revolved around tutoring, and during the process I learned a lot about source/version control, I gained a better understanding of how to utilize GitHub, and I got to dig in deep into Xcode and all of its features. I also gained experience using Swift.

Society of Women Engineers

SWE has enabled me to network and connect with other females in engineering. I believe having a strong support group as a female engineering student is important, and SWE also is very active in the community. Grade school students travel from all over Texas to attend some of SWEs events that promote engineering amongsts young females- such as their "Catch the Engineering Bug" event.

Alpha Kappa Psi

Alpha Kappa Psi is the word's oldest and largest professional business fraternity. Through Alpha Kappa Psi, I have been able to network with professionals across the board, I have developed essential leadership skills by holding chair positions and attending workshops, and my communication and public speaking skills have grown substantially.

National Society of Black Engineers

The mission of NSBE is "to increase the number of culturally responsible Black Engineers who excel academically, success professionally and positively impact the community." As a member of NSBE, I have been able to connect and collaborate with other African American Engineers at Texas Tech.

Genki Club

Genki Club is Texas Tech's Japanese language and culture club. As a member of Genki Club, I am able to learn more about traditional and modern Japanese values, as well as practice the Japanese language with certified tutors and professors.
